What happened

Day one is the whole game.

The 17 that never closed were not bad homes. They were not in worse condition. They were not on uglier streets. Same tract, same buyers shopping at the same time.

The split came from one decision: pricing and positioning on day one.

The first 14 days of a listing are the only 14 days you have leverage. After that, agents start treating it like inventory. Buyers start asking why it's still sitting. Every price cut from there costs more than it saved.

"Of the 17 Bridgeport homes that didn't sell, 10 cut their price at least once before giving up. Price wrong on day one, you pay for it all the way down."
